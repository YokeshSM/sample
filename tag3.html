THE STORK

About Stork

Stork, (family Ciconiidae), any of about 20 species of long-necked large birds constituting the family Ciconiidae (order Ciconiiformes), related to the herons, flamingos, and ibises. Storks range from about 60 cm to more than 150 cm (2 to 5 feet) in height.

Species

  • Genus Anastomus: two species
  • Genus Leptoptilos: three species
  • Genus Mycteria: four species
  • Genus Jabiru: one species
  • Genus Ephippiorhynchus: two species
  • Genus Ciconia: eight species

Features

Like all storks, it has long legs, a long neck and a long straight pointed beak. The sexes are identical in appearance, except that males are larger than females on average. The plumage is mainly white with black flight feathers and wing coverts; the black is caused by the pigment melanin.
